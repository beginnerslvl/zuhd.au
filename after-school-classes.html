Islamic after-school classes (Maktab) are supplementary educational programs offered to children and teenagers in many Muslim communities around the world. These classes typically take place outside of regular school hours and focus on teaching the principles of Islam, including the Quran, Hadith, Islamic history, Fiqh, & Aqidah.

The curriculum of Islamic after-school classes may vary depending on the age of the students and the focus of the program. In some cases, the classes may be designed to teach the fundamentals of Islam to young children, such as the Arabic alphabet and basic Islamic rituals like prayer and fasting. As the students get older, the curriculum may become more advanced, focusing on topics like Islamic ethics, jurisprudence, and spirituality.

Islamic after-school classes are often taught by volunteer teachers who are knowledgeable in Islamic studies and are passionate about sharing their knowledge with the next generation. The classes may be held in mosques, community centers, or other dedicated spaces.

In addition to providing students with a deeper understanding of Islam, these classes can also help to foster a sense of community among Muslim families. By attending these classes, children and teenagers can connect with their peers and develop relationships with older members of the community who can serve as mentors and role models.

Overall, Islamic after-school classes play an important role in the education and spiritual development of young Muslims, providing them with the knowledge and skills they need to navigate the challenges of modern life while remaining true to their faith.
